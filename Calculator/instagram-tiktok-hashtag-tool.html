The Ultimate Guide to Using Hashtags on Instagram and TikTok

Why Hashtags Matter for Social Media Success

Hashtags are more than just trendy additions to your posts—they're powerful tools that can dramatically increase your content's discoverability and engagement. On platforms like Instagram and TikTok, hashtags function as a categorization system that helps users find content related to specific topics, interests, or communities.

When you include relevant hashtags in your posts, you're essentially creating doorways that allow new audiences to discover your content. This increased visibility can lead to more likes, comments, shares, and followers, ultimately boosting your social media presence and influence.

Instagram Hashtag Strategy: Best Practices

Instagram allows up to 30 hashtags per post, but quantity doesn't always equal quality. Research suggests that posts with 9-12 hashtags tend to perform best in terms of engagement. When crafting your Instagram hashtag strategy, consider these best practices:

  • Mix hashtag sizes: Include a combination of popular (1M+ posts), moderately popular (100K-1M posts), and niche (less than 100K posts) hashtags to maximize your reach while ensuring your content doesn't get lost in oversaturated categories.
  • Use location-based hashtags: If your content is relevant to a specific location, include location-based hashtags to attract local audiences.
  • Create a branded hashtag: Develop a unique hashtag for your brand or campaign to encourage user-generated content and build community.
  • Research competitors: Analyze the hashtags used by successful accounts in your niche to identify effective hashtag strategies.
  • Update your hashtags regularly: Avoid using the same set of hashtags for every post. Rotate through different hashtag combinations to reach diverse audiences and prevent being flagged as spam.

TikTok Hashtag Strategy: What Works

TikTok's algorithm differs from Instagram's, and so does its approach to hashtags. While Instagram allows up to 30 hashtags, TikTok has a character limit for captions (including hashtags) of 100 characters. This means you need to be more strategic with your hashtag selection. Here's what works on TikTok:

  • Trending hashtags: TikTok's "Discover" page highlights trending hashtags and challenges. Including these in your content can significantly boost visibility.
  • Niche-specific hashtags: Use hashtags that are specific to your content category to reach users who are interested in your niche.
  • Broad and specific combination: Include a mix of broad hashtags (like #fyp or #foryoupage) and specific ones related to your content.
  • Participate in challenges: TikTok thrives on challenges. Using challenge-specific hashtags can help your content gain traction.
  • Quality over quantity: Due to character limitations, focus on 3-5 highly relevant hashtags rather than trying to include as many as possible.

The Science Behind Effective Hashtag Selection

Selecting the right hashtags isn't just about popularity—it's about relevance, timing, and strategic placement. Here's the science behind effective hashtag selection:

Relevance is Key

Always prioritize hashtags that accurately describe your content. Irrelevant hashtags might bring temporary traffic but can harm your account's credibility and algorithm performance in the long run.

Hashtag Competition

Extremely popular hashtags (with millions of posts) are highly competitive, making it difficult for your content to stand out. The sweet spot often lies in moderately popular hashtags that balance visibility with competition.

Timing Considerations

Some hashtags perform better at specific times or days. For instance, #MondayMotivation is most effective on Mondays, while #WeekendVibes works best on weekends.

Hashtag Analytics

Regularly analyze which hashtags drive the most engagement for your content and refine your strategy accordingly using platform insights and third-party analytics tools.

Common Hashtag Mistakes to Avoid

Even with the best intentions, creators often make hashtag mistakes that can limit their content's potential. Here are some common pitfalls to avoid:

Using banned hashtags

Some hashtags have been banned or restricted by platforms due to association with inappropriate content. Using these can limit your content's reach.

Hashtag stuffing

Adding irrelevant hashtags just to increase numbers can be perceived as spam by both users and algorithms.

Ignoring research

Failing to research hashtag performance and relevance before using them can result in missed opportunities for visibility.

Not tracking analytics

Not tracking which hashtags drive engagement for your specific content makes it impossible to refine your strategy.
